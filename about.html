Goal Overview

What is SDG 7?

SDG 7 is one of 17 United Nations Sustainable Development Goals adopted in 2015 to be reached by 2030.

lightbulb

Affordable, Reliable, Sustainable, Modern

SDG 7 aims to ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable, and modern energy services for every person on the planet. It recognises that energy is the foundation of education, healthcare, employment, and human dignity.

The goal sets out three big targets by 2030: universal access to electricity and clean cooking, a substantial increase in the share of renewable energy, and a doubling of the global rate of improvement in energy efficiency.

check_circle Target 7.1 — universal access to affordable, reliable energy services.
check_circle Target 7.2 — substantially increase the share of renewables.
check_circle Target 7.3 — double the rate of energy-efficiency improvement.
check_circle Target 7.A & 7.B — boost clean-energy R&D and infrastructure in developing nations.

Why It Matters to All of Us

Clean and affordable energy is not just an environmental goal — it is a quality-of-life goal. It powers classrooms, clinics, businesses, and the digital tools we use every day.

When students like us understand SDG 7, we can help spread awareness, encourage clean-energy choices, and adopt simple habits that reduce energy waste at home and in our communities.

Key Issues

The Energy Challenges We Face

These barriers prevent the world from achieving SDG 7 — and each one connects directly to the case for solar.

power_off

Unequal Access to Electricity

Hundreds of millions of people in rural and underserved communities still lack reliable electricity, which limits education, healthcare, and economic opportunity.

arrow_forward Solar mini-grids reach where the grid cannot.
local_gas_station

Reliance on Fossil Fuels

Coal, oil, and gas dominate global supply. They drive greenhouse-gas emissions, air pollution, and price shocks whenever fuel markets become volatile.

arrow_forward Solar produces zero direct emissions.
psychology

Low Awareness of Renewables

Many households do not know how solar works, what it costs, or how energy efficiency saves money. Awareness gaps slow adoption and waste resources.

arrow_forward Education unlocks demand and trust.
bolt

Energy Waste & High Bills

Inefficient appliances, poor insulation, and standby power inflate household bills. Wasted electricity also wastes the fuel and emissions used to generate it.

arrow_forward Efficiency is the cheapest energy of all.
public

Climate Change Pressure

Burning fossil fuels heats the planet, raises sea levels, and disrupts weather. Reaching SDG 7 directly supports SDG 13 (Climate Action).

arrow_forward Clean energy is climate action.
attach_money

Affordability Gaps

Up-front costs can put modern energy services out of reach for low-income households even where the grid is available.

arrow_forward Solar costs have fallen ~90% since 2010.
