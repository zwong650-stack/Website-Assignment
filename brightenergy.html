Goal Overview

What is SDG 7?

An introduction to the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 7 and why it matters for everyone.

lightbulb

Affordable, Reliable, Sustainable Energy

SDG 7 aims to ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable, and modern energy for all. Energy is essential for education, healthcare, business, communication, and daily life.

However, many communities still face limited electricity access, high energy costs, dependence on fossil fuels, and low awareness of energy-saving practices. Reaching SDG 7 means closing these gaps so no one is left behind.

Why It Matters to All of Us

Clean and affordable energy directly improves quality of life. It powers classrooms, hospitals, small businesses, and the digital tools we use every day.

By understanding SDG 7, students like us can help spread awareness, support clean energy choices, and adopt simple habits that reduce energy waste at home and in our communities.

Key Issues

Challenges We Face

These are some of the major barriers that prevent the world from achieving Sustainable Development Goal 7.

power_off

Unequal Access to Electricity

Some rural and underserved communities still have limited access to reliable electricity, which affects education, healthcare, communication, and economic opportunities.

local_gas_station

Dependence on Fossil Fuels

Many countries still rely on coal, oil, and gas for energy. These sources can increase greenhouse gas emissions and contribute to climate change and air pollution.

Low Energy Efficiency Awareness

Many people waste electricity because they are unaware of simple energy-saving habits or do not have access to efficient technologies.

Take Action

Simple Energy Saving Tips

Small, easy habits anyone can apply at home, school, or work to reduce energy waste and support SDG 7.

01

Switch off when not in use

Turn off lights, fans, and electronics when leaving a room to avoid wasting electricity.

02

Use natural daylight

Open curtains and use sunlight during the day instead of switching on indoor lights.

03

Unplug after use

Unplug chargers and small appliances when fully charged or finished to stop "phantom" energy use.

04

Choose efficient devices

Pick energy-efficient bulbs and appliances when possible — they use less power and last longer.

05

Use AC wisely

Set air conditioning to a moderate temperature (around 24°C) and clean filters regularly.

06

Support renewable energy

Choose, share, or support solar, wind, and other clean energy solutions where they are available.
