Step by Step

How Solar Energy Works

A photovoltaic (PV) system turns sunlight into the same kind of electricity that comes out of any wall socket. It does so in five clear steps.

1

Sunlight hits the panel

Solar panels are made of silicon cells. When photons from the sun strike them, they knock electrons loose — the photovoltaic effect.

2

DC electricity is generated

Those moving electrons flow through the cell as direct current (DC) electricity, the same kind that powers a battery.

3

Inverter converts to AC

An inverter changes DC into alternating current (AC) at 230 V, the type used by homes, schools, and most appliances.

4

You use it (or store it)

Power flows through your meter to your lights, fan, fridge, or laptop. Extra power can charge a battery for nighttime.

5

Surplus feeds the grid

If your system produces more than you need, the meter runs backwards and your provider credits you — called net energy metering.

6

Monitoring & maintenance

Modern systems include an app that tracks production daily. Routine cleaning is the only regular upkeep most homes need.

Solar & Wind Work Together

Solar produces the most electricity at midday on sunny days. Wind tends to peak in the evening, at night, and through cloudy or stormy periods. Combined, they cover a wider part of the day than either source alone.

That's why grids that go renewable usually invest in solar plus wind — with batteries to bridge the gaps. Together they form a near-24/7 clean energy system that can replace coal and gas plants.

Why Choose Solar

Six Solid Reasons to Go Solar

Each benefit ties back directly to one or more SDG 7 targets.

eco

Renewable & Endless

The sun delivers more energy to Earth in one hour than humanity uses in a whole year. Solar will not run out in any meaningful timeframe.

cloud_off

Clean & Low-Carbon

Producing solar power releases no smoke, no soot, and no greenhouse gases. The carbon footprint is mostly from manufacturing and transport.

savings

Lower Electricity Bills

Once installed, sunlight is free. Households that go solar typically save 50–90% on their monthly electric bill for 25 years or more.

cottage

Reaches Rural Areas

Off-grid panels and mini-grids power villages and farms where extending the main grid would be too expensive or slow.

build

Low Maintenance

No moving parts in the panels themselves. Routine cleaning and an occasional inverter check keep a system running for decades.

work

Creates Green Jobs

Solar already employs millions worldwide in installation, manufacturing, sales, and maintenance — many of them local roles.

Frequently Asked

Common Questions About Solar

If you've ever wondered any of these, you're not alone. Tap a question to see the answer.

Does solar work on cloudy or rainy days?
Yes — just less. On overcast days, panels typically produce 10–25% of their normal output. Rain even helps by washing dust off the glass. Real systems are sized to cover annual demand, not just sunny hours.
What happens at night?
The panels stop producing, but you can either draw from the grid (the meter runs forward) or use a battery you charged earlier in the day. Hybrid systems combine both for full 24-hour coverage.
How long do solar panels last?
Most panels carry a 25-year output warranty — meaning the manufacturer guarantees at least 80% of original output after 25 years. The actual physical lifespan often exceeds 30 years.
Is solar really cheaper than coal or gas?
In most countries, yes. The unsubsidised cost of new solar electricity has fallen below new coal and gas in over 90% of the world. That's why even oil-producing regions are building solar.
Can I install solar at home in Malaysia?
Yes. Programmes like NEM (Net Energy Metering) and SELCO let homeowners install rooftop solar and sell surplus electricity back to the grid. Payback is typically 5–8 years.
What about students who can't install panels?
You can still help. Energy efficiency at home, using daylight, supporting solar policy, and spreading awareness all reduce demand for fossil fuels — which is exactly the goal of SDG 7.
